---
title: "Being Review-Ready Before the Build Exists"
date: "2026-02-04"
excerpt: "Store review should not be treated as a final boss. Good teams design permissions, copy, screenshots, and compliance while building."
tags: ["Play Console", "App Store", "Release"]
---

I do not like discovering review problems after the build is uploaded. That is too late, and the delay feels very avoidable.

Review-readiness starts while the feature is still being built.

- **Permission copy comes early:** camera, location, NFC, Bluetooth, and notifications need a clear product reason.
- **Screenshots must match reality:** the store page should tell the same story as the current release.
- **Test accounts stay alive:** reviewers should never hit a dead login, expired OTP, or environment-only wall.
- **Privacy labels need engineering input:** product guesses are risky when SDKs, analytics, and storage are involved.
- **Rollback language is prepared:** if a rollout pauses, support and stakeholders should know exactly what changed.

The store is part of the product surface. Treating it like engineering work keeps release day calm and professional.
